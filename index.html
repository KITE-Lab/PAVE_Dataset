Overview

Why PAVE matters

Existing datasets such as KITTI, nuScenes, Waymo, Argoverse, nuPlan, and Zenseact Open Dataset (ZOD) mainly support perception tasks, while most are collected from manually driven vehicles.

PAVE is designed to close that gap with real-world logs captured under autonomous driving mode, enabling holistic evaluation of intelligent driving behavior, safety events, and trajectory quality.

Scenario Diversity

Scenario Coverage and Structured Annotation Schema

The PAVE dataset provides diverse real-world driving scenarios together with structured scenario-level annotations to support systematic condition-aware analysis, subset construction, and reproducible benchmark evaluation.

Scenario Annotation

Structured schema for scenario-level analysis

01

Area Type

Allowed values [highway, urban, residential, rural, parking, other]

  • highway: highways, urban expressways, elevated roads, ramps, toll stations, and other high-speed road segments
  • urban: city roads including arterial, secondary, and local streets with dense intersections and roadside buildings
  • residential: internal roads within residential areas, campuses, or industrial parks
  • rural: rural or suburban roads, including non-urban segments of national or provincial roads
  • parking: parking lots, underground garages, or designated parking areas
  • other: scenarios that cannot be clearly categorized into the above types
02

Lighting

Allowed values [day, dusk, dawn, night, night_lit, night_unlit, other]

  • day: daytime with sufficient natural light, including overcast daytime conditions
  • dusk: evening period around sunset with declining ambient illumination
  • dawn: early morning period with low natural light before full daylight
  • night: general nighttime conditions when illumination detail is not further distinguished
  • night_lit: nighttime with clear street lighting or other artificial illumination
  • night_unlit: nighttime with little or no artificial roadside lighting
  • other: unclear or special lighting conditions outside the standard categories
03

Weather

Allowed values [clear, cloudy, rain, snow, fog, other]

  • clear: sunny or stable conditions without precipitation
  • cloudy: overcast conditions with reduced direct sunlight
  • rain: active rainfall or post-rain conditions with visible wet-road effects
  • snow: snowfall or snow-covered road environments
  • fog: fog, haze, or reduced-visibility atmospheric conditions
  • other: unclear or special weather conditions not covered above
04

Road Surface

Allowed values [paved, unpaved, other]

  • paved: asphalt or concrete roads with regular on-road driving characteristics
  • unpaved: dirt roads, gravel roads, or temporary construction paths with unsealed surfaces
  • other: mixed or unclear surface types that cannot be reliably assigned
05

Driving State

Allowed values [stop, follow, cruise, lane_change, turn, other]

  • stop: the vehicle is stationary or nearly stationary, for example at a traffic light or stop line
  • follow: the vehicle is closely following a leading vehicle in the same lane
  • cruise: the vehicle maintains lane and speed without close leading-vehicle interaction
  • lane_change: the vehicle is actively changing lanes or crossing lane boundaries
  • turn: the vehicle is executing a turn or traversing a sustained curved path
  • other: insufficient visual evidence or behaviors not covered by the standard states

Sensor Setup

Synchronized sensing with high-precision localization

According to the paper, PAVE is built on a fixed multi-sensor platform with four high-resolution RGB cameras and a high-precision GNSS/IMU unit, enabling synchronized perception and trajectory capture in real autonomous-driving operation.

Camera Suite 4 RGB cameras

Front-wide (120°), front-tele (30°), left-wide (120°), and right-wide (120°).

Image Quality 2592 x 1944 @ 30 FPS

All cameras follow the same high-resolution recording setup.

Localization 20 Hz GNSS + 200 Hz IMU

GNSS is further enhanced by RTK differential correction via CORS.

Accuracy ~0.8 cm

Paper-reported localization accuracy for trajectory alignment.

Front RGB Cameras

The forward-view setup combines a front-wide camera for broad scene coverage and a front-telephoto camera for longer-range observation.

120° front-wide FOV, 30° front-telephoto FOV, and 2592 x 1944 capture at 30 FPS.

  • 120° wide FOV
  • 30° narrow FOV
  • 2592 x 1944 at 30 FPS

Side-wide Cameras

Left and right side cameras expand lateral coverage so the dataset can better capture surrounding traffic and road context.

Both side-wide cameras use 120° FOV and record at 2592 x 1944 resolution and 30 FPS.

  • 120° field of view
  • 2592 x 1944 at 30 FPS
  • Aligned with front-view capture

GNSS / IMU Module

The localization stack fuses GNSS and IMU measurements, while RTK differential correction through the CORS network improves position accuracy.

The paper reports 20 Hz GNSS, 200 Hz IMU, and approximately 0.8 cm localization accuracy.

  • 20 Hz GNSS
  • 200 Hz IMU
  • RTK-level precision

Synchronization & Output

GNSS time acts as the global temporal reference so that video streams and trajectory states stay aligned across the whole system.

Logged states include latitude, longitude, altitude, velocity, heading, roll, pitch, and yaw.

  • Shared reference clock
  • Cross-sensor temporal consistency
  • Reliable trajectory reconstruction
